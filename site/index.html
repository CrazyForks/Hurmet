This week’s example:

Spinning Thread

I’m reading Virginia Postrel’s The Fabric of Civilization. It’s a fascinating book that describes the technology of fabric and its history. I never knew, for instance, how long it takes to create thread using pre-modern techniques.

The need arises because natural fibers (other than silk) are short. A cotton fiber is 2 inches long at most and wool isn’t more than 6 inches. So fibers are spun into tightly wound threads. The people who did the spinning, by the way, were almost always women. After reading a description of the pre-modern techniques, I was impressed by how fast they could work.
