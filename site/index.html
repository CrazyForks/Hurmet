Adams and Exponential Growth

Henry Adams was a man of letters, but now and then a number would creep into his work. In Mont Saint Michelle and Chartres he wrote of architecture during the eleventh, twelfth, and thirteenth centuries and alluded several times to our “250 million arithmetical ancestors”. Can that be right?

Well, he published that book in the year 1904.

generations

, number of “arithmetical ancestors”

If anything, he understated the case. The calculation above is very sensitive to the denominator used to the find the number of generations. Twenty five years from birth to birth sounds high for an average.

In his memoir, The Education of Henry Adams, he wrote of the increase in available energy during his lifetime.

The coal-output of the world, speaking roughly, doubled every ten years between 1840 and 1900, in the form of utilized power, for the ton of coal yielded three or four times as much power in 1900 as in 1840.

That’s a about a 7% per year rate of increase.

Check:

J Storrs Hall[1] calls that phenomenon the “Henry Adams Curve” and fitted a curve to EIA data[2] to claim that such a rate held true from 1800 to about 1970.

[1] J Storrs Hall, Where is My Flying Car, 2017

[2] https://www.eia.gov/totalenergy/data/annual/perspectives.php
